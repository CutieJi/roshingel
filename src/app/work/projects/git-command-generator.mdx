## Overview

MISLend is a professional, high-performance web application designed for the UCC MIS Department (University of Caloocan City - Management Information Systems) to streamline the process of borrowing and returning technical equipment.

## Key Features

- **Instant QR Borrowing**: Scan physical QR codes on equipment for immediate tracking.
- **Real-time Availability**: Browse available projectors, laptops, networking kits, and more.
- **Personal Dashboard**: Track your current borrows, history, and return deadlines.
- **Simple Returns**: Hand back equipment and log it in seconds with condition reporting (Good/Damaged).
- **Dedicated Dashboard**: Optimized workflow for faculty members.
- **Equipment Reservation**: Ensure tools are ready for classroom sessions.
- **Account Approval**: Verify and approve student registrations before they can borrow.
- **Live Inventory Tracking**: Real-time counts of total, borrowed, and maintenance items.
- **QR Generation**: Generate and download unique QR codes for new hardware directly from the dashboard.
- **Automated Logging**: Export full transaction history to Excel/CSV for institutional audits.
- **Maintenance Queue**: Flag damaged items for repair and track their status.

## Technologies Used

- **HTML**: Used to structure the web pages and create the interactive interface for selecting Git actions.
- **CSS**: Applied for styling the interface, ensuring a clean, responsive, and user-friendly design.
- **Javascript**: Implemented the core functionality to dynamically borrow/return based on user inputs.
- **Firebase**: Built with a focus on ease of use, security, and administrative control, it leverages Firebase for real-time data synchronization and secure authentication, replacing traditional paper-based logs with a modern QR-driven solution.

## Challenges and Learnings

The MIS currently doesn’t use any digital platform or system that records and tracks equipment borrowing of students and professors, using a manual log book instead for borrowing processes, where students write down the name, their name, equipment borrowed and signature.

Through MISLend, students can browse through the available equipment first in the website, to prevent an unnecessary trip to the MIS. Monitoring and tracking of which student borrowed the equipment will be easier in the event that an equipment is due, not returned or came back damaged. The process of borrowing will be quicker and efficient through the website’s pre-borrowing system and QR code scanning.

## Outcome

The developed system, MISLend, provides a modern and centralized platform for managing equipment borrowing transactions within the school. The system allows administrators to efficiently monitor equipment availability, approve borrowing requests, manage users, and track borrowing history in real time. Through its dashboard interface, admins can quickly view statistics such as total equipment, currently borrowed items, pending approvals, and borrowing logs.

For students, the system offers a more convenient and organized borrowing process by allowing them to request equipment digitally instead of relying on manual paper-based transactions. The integration of QR code scanning, approval workflows, and automated record management improves transaction accuracy, reduces processing time, and minimizes the risk of lost records.

The expected outcome of the study is an improved equipment lending process that is faster, more accurate, secure, and easier to manage. The system also enhances accountability among borrowers and administrators by maintaining complete borrowing records and monitoring overdue returns. Overall, the implementation of the system is expected to increase operational efficiency and support better inventory management within the institution.
